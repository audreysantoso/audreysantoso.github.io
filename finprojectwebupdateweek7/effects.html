As the use of plastic increase, so does the waste. According to the Ministry of Environment and Forestry of The Republic of Indonesia (2021), Indonesia produces 28,5 million tons of trash annually, and 17% of them (4,85 million tons) come from plastics. Parts of these trash can turn into microplastic that polluts the freshwater. Microplastics can come from decays of larger plastic objects or small plastic bits such as from body scrubs and laundry detergent.

This website intends to spread awareness about microplastic, including its sources, process of being eaten by the fish, and the health effects on human. You can also make an impact on this issue by donating or signing petition of the Ecoton Organization. Ecoton is an Indonesian-based environment organization that focuses on river conservation.

Most Effective Thing to Do
Stop plastic use, especially single-use plastic

The Bare Minimum
Realize how much waste is produced from your daily habit

Other steps you can take

  • Buy organic clothes (or those made from organic materials)
  • Air-drying your laundry
  • Washing your clothing less often
  • Buy plastic-free cosmetics
  • Reduce shellfish consumption
  • Use public or alternative transportation
  • Don’t microwave your food in plastic
  • Regularly dust and vacuum
